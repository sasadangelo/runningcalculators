Heart Rate Zone

Calculate your training zone inserting your max and rest heart rate. The calculator will compute the training zone using the Karvonen formula. The resting heart rate is best measured when you first wake up in the morning, before your feet leave the sheets. Grab a clock or watch with a second hand, then find your pulse and count how many beats occur within 60 seconds. The shortcut to this method is to count the number of beats in 20 seconds, and then to multiply that number by 3. You can determine your maximum heart rate by putting on your running shoes, firing up your heart rate monitor and heading out into the real world. You won't need fancy laboratory equipment for the field test but youâ€™ll still get an accurate and personal estimation of your maximum heart rate. The premise is simple: you warm up properly and then do an exercise that brings you close to your maximum effort. For an example of field test, see the end of this article. If you do not know your maximum heart rate you can select the No option in the radio button and enter your age. The calculator will estimate your maximum heart rate using the Haskell & Foxit formula (220-age).
